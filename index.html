  • CHFA down payment assistance — Colorado Housing and Finance Authority programs designed to help with down payments and closing costs

Frequently Asked Questions About First-Time Home Buyer Programs

What first-time home buyer programs are available in Colorado?
Several programs exist for Colorado buyers, including CHFA down payment assistance programs, USDA Rural Development financing in eligible areas, and local housing initiatives. The programs available depend on your location, income, and the lender you work with. Many change annually, so it's important to explore your specific options.
Are there down payment assistance programs in Northern Colorado?
Yes. Down payment assistance programs exist through various sources including CHFA, local government initiatives, and lender programs. Eligibility varies, and the amount of assistance available depends on your financial situation, the property location, and which program you qualify for.
What credit score do I need to buy a home in Colorado?
Credit score requirements vary by loan program and lender. Some programs offer flexibility for buyers with lower credit scores, while others have stricter requirements. The best way to understand what's available for your situation is to discuss your profile with a knowledgeable lender.
Can USDA loans help me buy in Northern Colorado?
USDA Rural Development loans are one financing option that may allow qualified buyers to purchase a home with little or no down payment in certain eligible areas. Eligibility depends on location, income limits, and lender approval. Not all areas of Northern Colorado qualify as eligible rural areas under USDA guidelines.
How do I know which programs I might qualify for?
